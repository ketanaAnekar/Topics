The "Hello My Name Is" stickers are an iconic part of street art and graffiti culture, having roots in a variety of subcultures that range from corporate to creative. Originally designed for corporate settings or name-tag purposes, these stickers have been co-opted by graffiti artists and the wider street art community as a canvas for self-expression. The simplicity of the blank space combined with the implied invitation to introduce oneself makes them ideal for artists looking to leave their mark in urban environments. Over time, the "Hello My Name Is" stickers have evolved from simple labels into a platform for creativity, showcasing elaborate tags, illustrations, and sometimes even social or political messages

In the graffiti scene, these stickers became popular due to their convenience and the instant visibility they offer. Artists can prepare the stickers in advance, making it easy to place them quickly in various public spaces. This adaptability allows artists to create intricate designs in the safety of their own homes or studios and then distribute them across cities in seconds, avoiding the risks associated with traditional graffiti methods. The street art community appreciates how the stickers blur the lines between traditional graffiti, sticker art, and guerilla advertising. They often become part of a larger narrative within the streetscape, contributing to the dynamic visual dialogues happening in cities worldwide.

Hello My Name Is" stickers have transcended their original purpose, finding new life in artistic and cultural contexts. Some artists use these stickers to present bold and intricate lettering styles, turning what was once a plain name tag into an art form in itself. Others may employ the sticker's recognizable format to challenge social norms, disrupt commercial spaces, or send personal messages. The ubiquitous nature of these stickers allows them to infiltrate all kinds of public and private spaces, thus enabling artists to have a presence without resorting to more confrontational forms of graffiti.

The stickers' cultural significance has grown to the point where they are now seen not just as individual pieces but as part of the collective urban art tapestry. Their placement on various surfaces—whether it be walls, street signs, or subway cars—adds to the layered, collage-like quality of street art environments. Each sticker adds to a larger, ongoing conversation in public spaces, reflecting the diversity of voices and perspectives in the urban art scene. Some artists even trade these stickers, creating a network of shared art and collaboration that spans across cities and countries, further solidifying their role as cultural artifacts.
